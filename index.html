Biography

I am originally from southern New Mexico, but I currently reside in Arizona. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering (E.E.) from Arizona State University (ASU) under the direction of Professor Dan Bliss . My research focused on developing signal processing algorithms for software defined radio (SDR) with applications in wireless communications and radar. Before that, I received my B.S.E.E. and M.S.E.E. from New Mexico State University (NMSU). At NMSU my advisor was Professor Phillip De Leon and my thesis research investigated the advantages of utilizing graphics processing units (GPUs) for applications in speech signal processing, pattern recognition, and machine learning. I serve as a board member for the Si Se Puede Foundation and as a mentor for the Desert WAVE (Women in Autonomous Vehicle Engineering) robotics team. I also currently serve as a member of the Loyola Marymount University (LMU) Electrical and Computer Engineering Department (ECE) Advisory Board.

The views and opinions expressed here are completely my own and do not represent those of any organizations I am affiliated with.
